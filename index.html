ABOUT THE SYMPOSIUM

The Symposium on Chinese Archaeology and Cultural Research is a conference that focuses on the archaeology of the Yangzi River, one of the longest rivers in China and Asia. The conference is expected to bring together experts in the field of archaeology to share their research findings, discuss new discoveries, and exchange ideas on the cultural heritage and history of the Yangzi River.

The conference is hosted by Wuhan University, one of the top universities in China, and is scheduled to take place in 2023. The Yangzi River has a rich history that spans over several thousand years, and the archaeological discoveries in the area provide valuable insights into the development of ancient Chinese civilization, including the prehistoric cultures, Shang and Zhou dynasties, and the Han and Tang dynasties.

The conference aims to explore the different aspects of Yangzi River archaeology, including the material culture, religious practices, trade and commerce, and the role of the river in the social and political organization of ancient Chinese societies. The symposium also hopes to foster international collaborations and encourage the participation of scholars from around the world to promote academic exchange and knowledge sharing.

Overall, Symposium on Chinese Archaeology and Cultural Research will serve as a platform for researchers and scholars to discuss the latest archaeological findings and explore the significance of the Yangzi River in the development of ancient Chinese civilization.
